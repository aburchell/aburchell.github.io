Hi, I'm Gus! This is the living workspace that I call my website.

In terms of usual, around-the-circle, get to know you questions, I am a rising Senior (Class of 2020) at Swarthmore College. At Swat I am pursing a double major in computer science and cognitive science, and am part of the improv comedy group on campus, Vertigo-go. I've also dabbled in student government and ultimate frisbee, but so far those haven't drawn me as significantly as the activities with which I now fill my time.

More broadly, I am excited by the boundary between the human experience and computation, and by narratives of all forms. These fascinations well up in areas like natural language processing, computation creativity, and human-computer interaction.

Professionally, I am currently exploring the world of education through a Teaching Experiences for Undergraduates (TEU) at Trinity College. In this program, a group of fellow undergraduate interns and I do our best to get a group of rising highschool sophomore excited about science and inquiry by facilitating a summer science program.

I am also a research assistant in Denis Pelli's Object Recognition lab at New York University's Department of Psychology. Working with Dr. Pelli has been an incredible experience, presenting a baffling number of opportunities including getting my visual cortext retinotopically mapped using fMRI, expanding my knowledge of statistical analysis and data visualization, and presenting a talk about the possible link between the phenomenon of crowding and the size of various visual cortex areas at the Vision Science Society's (VSS) 2019 annual meeting.
